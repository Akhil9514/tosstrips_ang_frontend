What to expect on a Sailing adventure

  • A VIP experience

    Experience the luxury of sailing the Mediterranean on your own private yacht, discovering hidden gems accessible only by sea. Picture yourself unwinding on the deck of a secluded island cove, cruising to a private white-sand beach, and diving into warm ocean waters with no one else around—pure bliss!

  • All-in-one adventure

    Torn between a cultural adventure and an island-hopping escape? With a sailing tour, you don’t have to pick. The best itineraries blend rich cultural experiences, local flavors, exciting activities, and even wildlife encounters—like meeting the gentle giants of the Galápagos Islands.

  • New day, new destination

    Explore some of the world’s most pristine destinations, from Australia’s Great Barrier Reef to the Seychelles archipelago in Africa. With tens of thousands of islands framed by untouched golden beaches, there’s no better reason to embark on a sailing adventure. Just imagine stepping onto a new beach every single day!

  • Just escape

    Dreaming of a getaway but unsure where to go? A sailing holiday is the perfect escape from everyday life, where blue skies and refreshing waters create a tranquil home away from home. Let the gentle rhythm of the waves carry you to the island paradise you’ve always imagined.

Your Sailing trip questions, answered

  • Do I need to know how to sail?

    On most of our sailing adventures, a skipper or crew members will be on board to guide you, so no prior sailing experience is needed. If you’re interested in taking the helm yourself, some private sailing trips offer the chance to captain your own boat—just be sure to check with your operator to see if this option is available.

  • Am I required to help out on the boat?

    Sailing adventure operators come with a fully skilled crew, so you can relax without needing to pitch in—unless you’re eager to sharpen your sailing skills, of course!

  • What are the cabins like?

    Cabin sizes vary depending on the vessel, but they’re generally compact. Couples usually have their own cabin, while solo travelers may share with another passenger of the same gender or pay a single supplement for a private room. Most cabins feature ensuite bathrooms, though some boats may have shared facilities. If you’re unsure, your operator can provide all the details.

  • How much time is spent off the boat?

    Sailing tours perfectly balance life on and off the water. A typical day might start with waking up on board, taking a refreshing morning swim, then docking at a historic port to explore charming streets, sample local cuisine, and enjoy some free time before returning to your boat for the evening. Each sailing itinerary is unique, but most include guided excursions and a variety of land-based activities along the way.
