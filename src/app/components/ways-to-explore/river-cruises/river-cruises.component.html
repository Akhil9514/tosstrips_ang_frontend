Best ways to cruise

From family-friendly ocean liners to intimate small-ship expeditions and romantic river cruises, organized
water adventures offer the perfect vacation experience.

River Cruises

Swap the paved roads for the natural beauty of winding waterways on a river cruise. Explore multiple destinations in one seamless journey—no need to haul your suitcase from port to port. Treat yourself to a luxurious wine-tasting cruise or opt for a budget-friendly trip packed with delightful onshore excursions.

Polar Cruises

These bucket-list cruises offer the perfect way to unwind after a day spent exploring some of the planet’s coldest frontiers. From the Arctic to the Antarctic, polar expedition cruises provide an all-inclusive journey to remote, icy landscapes—complete with expert lectures, extraordinary wildlife encounters, and breathtaking excursions that will leave you speechless.

Ocean Cruises

Sail in style with a luxurious presidential suite or keep it simple and affordable in a cozy porthole cabin—ocean cruises offer accommodations for every budget. With a wealth of amenities, gourmet restaurants, relaxing spas and pools, plus daily entertainment, it’s no wonder these ships are often called floating hotels.

What to expect on a River Cruise

Entertainment and enrichment

Enjoy endless opportunities for entertainment and learning aboard your cruise. Catch captivating theatrical performances or attend insightful lectures that deepen your understanding of the destinations you’ll explore.

Exclusive can’t miss events

Luxury cruises offer exclusive access to unforgettable cultural experiences—from authentic Austrian Heurigen wine taverns and front-row opera seats to seasonal delights like the enchanting Christmas Markets.

Hassle-free shore excursions

Tailored to a wide range of interests, shore excursions include everything from hiking and taking in breathtaking views from the Rock of Lorelei to enjoying a meal at a Parisian restaurant overlooking the Eiffel Tower.

Your River Cruise trips questions, answered

  • When will I receive my travel documents?

    You can expect to receive your travel documents approximately 3-4 weeks prior to departure once you have paid for your cruise. Your documents usually include eTicket receipts, itineraries, and other essential information for your adventure.

  • When and where do I board the ship?

    All embarkation details are included within your final travel documents. Typically boarding starts at 3:00 pm to allow the crew enough time to prepare your stateroom and get the ship ready for your cruise. Weather, water levels, and ship scheduling can influence the final docking location for your ship, so please wait until you receive your final travel documents before you arrange flights or accommodations. .

  • What facilities will be in my room?

    Most staterooms feature a good-sized bed, towels, hairdryers, a writing desk, charging ports, and ample storage space. Some ships also have laundry facilities onboard for an extra charge. Of course, these can vary from room to room and ship to ship, so please check out the Ship Details on your adventure—or contact your tossntrips Cruise Consultant—to see the full list of inclusions.

  • Are shore excursions and meals included?

    Most cruises on tossntrips include breakfast, lunch, and dinner served on board, with the exception of only a few operators. Depending on your river cruise operator, shore excursions may be included in the cost of your adventure. Please contact your tossntrips Cruise Consultant for a full list of inclusions.

  • Are transfers to and from the port included?

    ISome river cruise operators include complimentary arrival and departure transfers within your total tour cost. If transfers are not included, please contact your tossntrips Cruise Consultant to purchase airport transfers.

  • Can I take a wheelchair onboard?

    Some ships are unable to accommodate travelers in wheelchairs due to difficulties with embarking and disembarking the ship, no onboard elevators, and narrow doorways on board. For travelers who use a collapsible wheelchair, they must be stored in the stateroom, which can sometimes be a tight squeeze. Please contact your tossntrips Cruise Consultant for more information.
