What to expect on a safari tour

  • Delve into local culture

    Beyond the wildlife, a safari tour brings you face-to-face with the guardians of these creatures—the local communities. Connecting with indigenous people is an essential part of the journey! What tales will the Maasai of Kenya share with you? Will spotting a tiger in its natural habitat move you to join a traditional dance as the sun sets over Northern India?

  • Witness geological wonders

    Escape the hustle of everyday life and step into a world of awe-inspiring beauty. Picture soaring above the stunning volcanic terrain of the Galapagos Islands, home to untouched wildlife and rare natural wonders found nowhere else on Earth.

  • Stay under starry skies

    After a day spent exploring the wilderness, there’s no better way to connect with fellow wildlife lovers than sharing a meal together as night falls. Unwind in a cozy tent or a luxury lodge beneath a clear, star-studded sky, far from city lights, where the night sparkles like never before.

  • Face-to-face encounters

    For a thrilling close-up with magnificent wildlife like lions, this is the ultimate adventure. You’ve seen documentaries showcasing incredible animals in their natural environment—now it’s your turn to experience it firsthand! A game drive gives you the chance to venture into the wild and create unforgettable memories just steps away from nature’s most spectacular creatures.

Join an epic Maasai Mara safari tour

If spotting the Big Five is on your bucket list, this Kenyan reserve is sure to deliver! Renowned for its incredible population of big cats—lions, leopards, and cheetahs—it’s also a sanctuary for black rhinos, elephants, wildebeest, and buffalo.

Fancy a hot air balloon ride soaring over the endless savanna? How about a champagne breakfast with panoramic views of the African plains? Multiple game drives through one of the world’s top safari destinations? You’ll find all this and more in the Maasai Mara.

And let’s not forget—“the Mara” is the stage for the Great Migration, one of the most breathtaking wildlife spectacles on the planet. Get ready for a front-row seat to nature’s greatest show!

Top destinations for safari tours

South Africa

If it’s your first time planning a safari, South Africa is an excellent choice. It offers abundant wildlife viewing alongside vibrant urban culture. With options ranging from self-drive adventures to private guided tours, it’s a perfect destination for families and couples alike.

Tanzania

Tanzania’s Northern Circuit, featuring the iconic Serengeti National Park and the breathtaking Ngorongoro Crater, is ideal for a safari adventure to spot the Big Five—perfectly capped off with a serene beach retreat in Zanzibar.

Kenya

Kenya, a prime destination for the Great Migration, boasts wildlife-packed national parks and stunning natural beauty. It’s an excellent choice if you want to pair your safari with a relaxing beach getaway or an adventurous climb up Kilimanjaro just across the border in Tanzania.

Botswana

Seasoned safari travelers often seek out more remote parks or choose exclusive experiences like a cruise through the Okavango Delta or a fly-in safari, offering unparalleled 360° aerial views of Africa’s wildlife.

Safari tours around the world

  • Africa

    When you picture a safari, Africa is usually the first place that comes to mind. The continent offers two distinct safari experiences: the vast savannahs where you can see lions, elephants, and the iconic Big Five, and the lush jungles of Uganda and Rwanda, home to our close relatives—the gorillas and chimpanzees.

  • Asia

    Asia is home to some of the planet’s most unique and rare wildlife. Encounter the elusive Bengal tiger in India’s Ranthambore or Nepal’s Chitwan National Parks, visit elephant sanctuaries deep in Thailand’s jungles, or witness the world’s largest lizard, the Komodo dragon, in Indonesia.

  • Australia

    Australia is a nature lover’s paradise. Stay at remote wilderness lodges, camp under the vast skies of the rugged Outback, hike around the iconic Uluru (Ayers Rock), spot kangaroos bounding across Kangaroo Island, or visit an animal sanctuary for a chance to cuddle adorable, teddy bear-like koalas.
